Collection Development

As a children's librarian specializing in collection development, my role is to curate a collection of materials that meet the unique needs and interests of the young patrons in my community. One of my primary goals is to ensure that our collection is diverse, inclusive, and representative of the children we serve. To achieve this, I regularly conduct needs assessments, survey our patrons, and solicit feedback from teachers, parents, and other community members. I also stay up-to-date on emerging trends and topics in children's literature, and work to add materials that feature characters and themes that reflect a range of cultures, backgrounds, and experiences. It is my belief that a diverse and inclusive collection is essential to fostering a love of reading and learning in children, and I take great pride in my work to ensure that our collection meets this goal.


Leadership Advocacy

I recognized the importance of creating a space for teens to have a voice in the services and programming we offer. That's why I decided to create a teen advisory board for our public library. I worked to recruit a diverse group of teens from the community who were passionate about reading, learning, and advocating for their peers. Through regular meetings and brainstorming sessions, we were able to collaborate on new programming ideas, suggest materials to add to our collection, and provide feedback on the services we offer. Not only did this help us to better serve our teen patrons, but it also helped to empower these young adults and give them a sense of ownership over their library. It was incredibly rewarding to see the impact that this advisory board had on our programming and services, and I believe it will continue to be a valuable resource for years to come.
